Ensure consistency and predictability in your processes.

What is SPC?

Statistical Process Control is an industry standard, data-driven methodology for measuring, controlling and improving process quality. Measurements are taken during the process and are analysed to determine two factors:

  • Control - Is the process output stable and consistent?
  • Capability - Does the process output meet your specification?

Where is SPC used?

It's widely used in manufacturing processes, but SPC can be applied to any industry where it's important to reduce waste, increase operational efficiency, and constantly strive to exceed customer expectations. When a measurement can be taken during a process or activity, SPC can be applied.

How to carry out SPC?

Statistical Process Control requires measurement data to be collected. These measurements relate to the quality of the finished product or service. The measurements are plotted on a control chart, with predetermined limits based on a sample of the data. Any data that falls within the control limits indicate that everything is operating as expected. However, if any data falls outside of these limits, it indicates that a special reason is causing variation outside of the normal range, and action should be taken to prevent defects from occurring. SPC measurements should be collected in real-time, so you can react to problems as quickly as possible.

Where to start with SPC for your company?

As a business it is important to consider the return on investment you will get from implementing an SPC solution. Start by considering common areas of waste and inefficiency. This may include poor manufacturing yields, quality control testing and inspection procedures, low service delivery scores, high levels of scrap or rework activities, existing data collection systems that are expensive and over complicated, paper based quality systems and reasons for customer returns. Often inefficiencies are not always obvious, and SPC can highlight areas of pain which you may not have had visibility of before.
