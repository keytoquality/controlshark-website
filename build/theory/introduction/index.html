Learning Introduction

ControlShark improves your organisation's quality and performance by carrying out Process Control and Process Capability analysis.

  • Process Control tells you how stable the spread (or variation) of the process is over time
  • Process Capability tells you how repeatable the process is at producing an output that meets the required specification

The above terms are often misunderstood, so it is important to understand the difference. For example, a process can be unstable, but at the same time it can be capable of meeting the required specification. This would be experienced as an unreliable process that could go wrong at any moment. As a general rule we should always ensure a process is stable before assessing its capability, to avoid this situation.

key point

By combining Process Control and Process Capability analysis, the goal is to ensure that the process is capable of consistantly producing the required output. In other words, get it right first time everytime.

This section takes you through the theory and execution behind both of the above aspects in detail. The content is not specific to ControlShark, and it is provided here as free training material for anyone to use.
